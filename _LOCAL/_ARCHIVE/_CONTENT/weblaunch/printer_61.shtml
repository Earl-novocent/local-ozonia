Air Preparation Units Oxygen Generators: PSA or VSA
Aug 9, 2004, 14:25

Air used as the feed gas for the production of ozone must have a high degree of purity. Therefore atmospheric air must be treated before it can be used. Oxygen can be generated on site by means of a Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) or Vacuum Swing Adsorption (VSA) plant or even a combination of both VPSA.

The requirements placed on such a treatment plant are:

  • Removal of dust particles from the air
  • Compression of the air to the required operating pressure
  • Drying of the air to a residual humidity corresponding to an atmospheric dew point of at least minus 60°C
  • The treatment of the air to this standard is essential for the reliable and efficient production of ozone.

Depending on the ozone capacity the air preparation will be designed as either a low or medium pressure unit. This comprises compressor(s), after cooler(s), refrigerant dryer(s), filters and heatless or heat regenerated adsorber dryer.

The adsorption technology is based on a physical separation process which uses the different adsorption properties of gases to a microporous solid substance, the so-called adsorbent. Nitrogen, for example, has a higher adsorption capacity to some zeolite molecular sieves than oxygen and can therefore be effectively separated from the air.

The main equipment of a PSA plant comprises an air compressor and two adsorber vessels filled with a drying agent and molecular sieve. Each adsorber operates on an alternating cycle of adsorption and regeneration, thus a continuous flow of oxygen is achieved.
